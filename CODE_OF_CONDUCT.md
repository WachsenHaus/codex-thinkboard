# Code of conduct

Thinkboard follows the [Contributor Covenant 3.0](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/3/0/code_of_conduct/) principles.

## Our commitment

We commit to a community where people can question ideas without attacking people. Different communication styles, experience levels, identities, and forms of uncertainty are welcome.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and open to correction.
- Critique ideas and code, not a contributor's character.
- Make room for incomplete thoughts and beginner questions.
- Protect private information encountered in examples, logs, or boards.
- Accept moderation decisions made to keep the project safe and constructive.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, doxxing, deliberate disclosure of private board content, sustained disruption, and retaliation against a reporter are not tolerated.

## Reporting and enforcement

For confidential conduct reports, use the repository's private reporting form and prefix the title with `Conduct:`:

https://github.com/WachsenHaus/codex-thinkboard/security/advisories/new

Do not put sensitive details in a public issue. Maintainers will review reports in good faith, protect reporter privacy as far as possible, and may edit or remove content, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban contributors depending on severity and pattern.

GitHub-hosted abuse may also be reported through GitHub's own content-reporting tools.
